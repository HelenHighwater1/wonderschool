New York State family child care licensing: Licensing process
By Wonderschool
By
How to use this guide
First of all, congratulations on taking this exciting step in your licensing journey! Trust us, we know how confusing state websites can be. Hopefully this guide will demystify some of the requirements.
Keep this guide close and check things off as you go ✅
Make use of our
library of resources
for additional support
Join Wonderschool for additional
community support
Step 1: OCFS Online Orientation
Complete the online Office of Children and Family Services Orientation
here
Use our tips
here
to help you get through the Orientation and request your application booklet
Step 2: Fill out Family Child Care Application Booklet
AND
Register for your CPR and First Aid Courses
You will have 90 days from the date your application booklet is issued to you, so get working on it as soon as you receive it
Search with your zip code
here
for CPR and First Aid courses near you - spots fill up quickly, so register as soon as you get your application booklet
You will need to submit CPR and First Aid certificates along with your booklet
Make sure to include a check to pay for the Statewide Central Registry (SCR) background check
It is $25 per person, and you will need a background check for yourself and each member of your family who is 18 years or older
If you need to request a new application booklet because you’ve moved/changed addresses, follow our guide
here
.
Step 3: Register for and complete your Health and Safety Training
Search with your zip code
here
for Health and Safety Training courses near you
You do not need to complete your Health and Safety Training before submitting your application booklet, but you do need a certificate of completion in time for your inspection with a licensing analyst.
Step 4: Make an appointment for fingerprinting for yourself and your family members
Call the phone number included in the fingerprinting information section in your application booklet to schedule an appointment
Step 5: Complete OCFS Fire/Safety & Licensor Inspections
An OCFS representative will complete an inspection of your program to ensure it meets necessary standards
Step 6: Success!
Pat yourself on the back, take a deep breath, and get some rest-- the real work is just beginning!
Minimum Requirements
You must have EITHER (check one)
2 years of paid or unpaid work experience caring for children under 6 years of age (including your own)
OR
1 year of paid or unpaid experience caring for children under 6 years of age and 6 hours of training or education in early childhood development
Authorization for a
background check
to be conducted on your behalf, as part of the application packet;
passing the background check for all adults over 18+ residing in the home where the family child care will be operating in
Medical clearance, basic physical from a medical provider (doctor, not registered nurse) that allows you to perform the typical duties of a child care provider (
see page 41
)
The medical provider is required to perform a TB test and sign off on your results
Other members in your household also needs to get a medical provider to sign off that members are healthy and can live in the household where children will be present for the daycare (
see page 67
)
